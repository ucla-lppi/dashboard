The percentage of census tracts classified as “disadvantaged communities” under California’s Senate Bill (SB 535). Disadvantaged communities, as defined by the California Environmental Protection Agency, are identified based on a combination of environmental pollution burden and population characteristics. Under SB 535, California’s Cap-and-Trade Program revenue is partly directed toward these communities through the California Climate Investments program to reduce pollution, enhance climate resilience, and improve health and economic well-being. The state most recently updated the list of disadvantaged communities in 2022. See more detailed information [online](https://oehha.ca.gov/calenviroscreen/sb535).
