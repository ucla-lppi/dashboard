Our Data

This section and our Frequently Asked Questions [LINK] answer many common questions about the methods and indicators used to develop the Latino Climate and Health Dashboard. Please refer to the technical report [LINK] for more information on our methods and data sources.

Age Groups 🔥 Demographic Indicators, Vulnerable Populations Description: The average percentage of individuals under the age of 18 and over the age of 65. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Age Groups 💨 Demographic Indicators, Vulnerable Populations Description: The average percentage of children under the age of 5 and adults over 65. Both of these age groups are more vulnerable to pollution-related conditions. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Annual Number of Extreme Heat Days (2018-2022) 🔥 Heat Exposure Description: The average number of days in a calendar year where the daily temperature reached a minimum of 90°F. We averaged data across 5 years, from 2018 to 2022. See detailed information online and for more information on how we constructed this indicator, please refer to our technical report. Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: National Environmental Public Health Tracking Network, 2023

Asthma🔥💨 Health Outcomes & Conditions Description: The average percentage of adults over the age of 18 who report they have been told by a doctor, nurse, or other health professional that they have asthma. The 2023 release of PLACES reflects data from 2021. See more detailed information online. Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: PLACES: Local Data for Better Health, 2023

Cleanup Sites 💨 Social Determinants of Health, Environmental Hazards Description: The average exposure score to cleanup sites. Exposure scores take into account the number of sites and facilities and their proximity to neighborhoods. Higher scores indicate greater exposure to cleanup sites. Data on cleanup sites are from 2020. See more detailed information online.

Clunker Vehicles 💨 Social Determinants of Health, Environmental Hazard Description: The average percent of registered “clunker” vehicles. Clunker vehicles, or vehicles 20 years or older, emit pollutants at higher levels because they typically lack advanced emission-control equipment. Data are from 2017 and reflect vehicles with model years from 1997 or earlier. See more detailed information online. Source: California Neighborhood Knowledge/California Air Resources Board (CNK-CARB) Transportation Disparity Tool, 2022

Coronary Heart Disease 🔥💨 Health Outcomes & Conditions Description: The average percentage of adults over the age of 18 who report they have been told by a doctor, nurse, or other health professional that they have coronary heart disease. The 2023 release of PLACES reflects data from 2021. See more detailed information online. Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: PLACES: Local Data for Better Health, 2023

Diabetes 🔥💨 Health Outcomes & Conditions Description: The average percent of adults over the age of 18 who report being told by a doctor or other health professional that they have diabetes. The 2023 release of PLACES reflects data from 2021. See more detailed information online. Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: PLACES: Local Data for Better Health, 2023

Diesel Particulate Matter (PM) 💨 Air Pollutants Description: The average annual concentration of diesel particulate matter emissions in tons. Data are from 2016. Diesel PM is the particle phase of exhaust, commonly known as “soot,” and is produced by diesel engines commonly used to power trucks, buses, cars, ships, trains, and heavy-duty equipment. See more detailed information online Source: CalEnviroScreen 4.0, 2021

Disadvantaged Communities 🔥💨 Vulnerable Populations, Social Determinants of Health Description: The percent of census tracts classified as “disadvantaged communities” under California’s Senate Bill (SB 535). Disadvantaged communities, as defined by the California Environmental Protection Agency, are identified based on a combination of environmental pollution burden and population characteristics. Under SB 535, revenue from California’s Cap-and-Trade Program is directed in part toward these communities through the California Climate Investments program to reduce pollution, enhance climate resilience, and improve health and economic well-being. The list of disadvantaged communities was most recently updated in 2022. See more detailed information online. Source: CalEnviroScreen 4.0, 2021

Emergency Department Visits for Heart Attacks 🔥💨 Health Outcomes & Conditions and Health Care Indicators Description: The average rate of emergency department visits per 10,000 people for heart attacks. Data on emergency department visits reflect a 3-year average from 2015-2017. See more detailed information online. Source: CalEnviroScreen 4.0, 2021

Emergency Department Visits for Asthma Attacks 🔥💨 Health Outcomes & Conditions and Health Care Indicators Description: The average rate of emergency department visits per 10,000 people for asthma attacks. Data on emergency department visits reflect a 3-year average from 2015-2017. See more detailed information online. Source: CalEnviroScreen 4.0, 2021

Fair/Poor Health Status 🔥💨 Social Determinants of Health, Health Outcomes & Conditions Demographic Indicator Description: The percentage of individuals who report their health as “fair” or “poor”. Data for fair or poor health status is self-reported and reflects individuals’ perceptions of their overall health. For this indicator, we pooled data over the 2018-2022 period to align with the 5-year American Community Survey methodology. See more detailed information on how this indicator is constructed online. Source: California Health Interview Survey, 2024

Food Insecurity 🔥💨 Social Determinants of Health Demographic Indicator Description: The percentage of households that lack consistent access to enough food for an active, healthy life. This measure draws from multiple data sources to account for economic and social conditions at the household level that limit food access. See more detailed information on how this indicator is constructed online. Source: Feeding America, 2022

Hazardous Waste Facilities 💨 Social Determinants of Health, Environmental Hazards Air Pollution Indicators Description: The average exposure score to hazardous waste facilities. Exposure scores take into account the number of sites and facilities and their proximity to neighborhoods. Higher scores indicate greater exposure to hazardous waste facilities. Data on hazardous waste facilities are from 2018 to 2020. See more detailed information online. Hazardous waste facilities include treatment, storage, and disposal sites which can release toxic substances such as carcinogens, mercury, and asbestos in the air, water, and soil. Source: CalEnviroScreen 4.0, 2021

Heat-Related Emergency Department Visits 🔥 Health Outcomes & Conditions Extreme Heat Indicators Description: The average rate of emergency visits per 10,000 persons related to extreme heat. The rate of heat-related visits to the emergency department covers the time period from 2009 through 2018. Source: UCLA Heat Maps, 2022

Impervious Surfaces 🔥 Social Determinants of Health Description: The average percent of land covered by impervious surfaces in 2021. These include man-made surfaces, such as roads, sidewalks, and buildings. See more detailed information online. Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: National Environmental Public Health Tracking Network, 2023

Latino and Non-Latino White 🔥💨 Demographic Indicator Description: The number and percentage of individuals who identify as Latino and non-Latino white. In this analysis, data for Latinos include individuals of any race, while data for white populations are limited to non-Latino white people. For more information on how “Latino” and “non-Latino white” are defined, please visit our FAQ page [LINK]. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Life Expectancy 🔥💨 Social Determinants of Health, Health Outcomes & Conditions Description: The average number of years an individual is expected to live from birth based on current mortality rates. Life expectancy is an age-adjusted indicator, ensuring fair comparisons across counties with differing age structures. Data for this indicator reflects a 3-year average (2019-2021) produced by County Health Rankings & Roadmaps. See more detailed information online. Source: County Health Rankings & Roadmaps, 2024

Limited English Proficiency 🔥💨 Demographic Indicators, Social Determinants of Health Description: The percentage of individuals ages 5 and over who are Limited English Proficient (LEP). LEP refers to persons who speak English less than very well. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Longest Period of Consecutive Extreme Heat Days (2022) 🔥 Heat Exposure Description: The average number of consecutive days where the daily temperature reached a minimum 90°F in 2022. See more detailed information online. Source: U.S. Census Bureau, Community Resilience Estimates for Heat, 2022

Low Birth Weight (LBW) Babies 💨 Vulnerable Populations, Health Outcomes & Conditions Description: The percentage of babies born weighing less than 5.5 pounds. LBW increases the risk of infant mortality, developmental delays, and chronic health conditions. Data on LBW reflect a 7-year average from 2009 to 2015. Source: CalEnviroScreen 4.0, 2021

Lower Emission Vehicles 💨 Social Determinants of Health, Environmental Hazards Description: The average percent of registered lower-emission vehicles. Lower-emission vehicles that use battery electric, plug-in hybrid, or hybrid technology to help reduce greenhouse gas emissions. Data are from 2017 and reflect vehicles with 2013 to 2017 model years. See more detailed information online. Source: California Neighborhood Knowledge/California Air Resources Board (CNK-CARB) Transportation Disparity Tool, 2022

Median Age 🔥💨 Demographic Indicators Description: The median population age in years. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Median Income (Household) 🔥💨 Social Determinants of Health Description: The median value of household income, defined by the combined money income of all household members ages 15 and over during the previous year. Amounts are expressed in 2022 dollar values. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Noncitizen Population 🔥💨 Demographic Indicators, Vulnerable Populations, Social Determinants of Health Description: The percentage of individuals residing in the U.S. who were not citizens at birth and were not citizens at the time of the population survey. The noncitizen population includes lawful permanent immigrants (or green-card holders), refugees and asylees, certain legal nonimmigrants (including those on student, work, or some other temporary visas), and persons residing in the country without authorization. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Obesity 🔥💨 Health Outcomes & Conditions Description: The average percent of adults over the age of 18 who are classified as obese. Obesity is defined as having a body mass index (BMI) greater than or equal to 30.0 kg/m² and is calculated using self-reported weight and height. The 2023 release of PLACES reflects data from 2021. See more detailed information online. Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: PLACES: Local Data for Better Health, 2023

Older Housing Units (built pre-1970) 🔥 Social Determinants of Health Description: The average percentage of housing units built before 1970. These units often lack modern features that mitigate heat exposure, such as central air conditioning, effective insulation, and energy-efficient windows. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Particulate Matter 2.5 (PM2.5) 💨 Air Pollutants Description: The average annual concentration of PM2.5 in micrograms per cubic meter (µg/m³). This measure represents data averaged over 3 years, from 2015 to 2017. Particulate matter is a mixture of aerosolized solid and liquid particles that include organic chemicals, dust, allergens, and metals. See more detailed information online. Source: CalEnviroScreen 4.0, 2021

Poverty Rate 🔥💨 Social Determinants of Health Description: The number and percentage of individuals living in poverty. The Census Bureau’s poverty threshold for a given year varies depending on the number of adults and children in a family and is adjusted annually for inflation. For example, the average poverty threshold for a family of four with two children under 18 was $29,678 in 2022. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Projected Number of Extreme Heat Days by Mid-Century (2035-2064) 🔥 Heat Exposure Description: The projected average number of days that will exceed 90°F in a calendar year during the mid-21st century (2035-2064). These projections are based on global climate models. See more detailed information online.
Source: California Healthy Places Index 3.0, 2022

Renter Households 🔥💨 Demographic Indicators, Social Determinants of Health Description: The percentage of occupied housing units that are rented rather than owned. These units include units leased under traditional renter agreements or life care arrangements and units occupied without payment, such as those provided by friends or relatives or in exchange for services (e.g. resident manager, caretaker). Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Risk Management Plan (RMP) Facilities 💨 Social Determinants of Health, Environmental Hazards Description: The average proximity score to RMP facilities. Proximity scores take into account the number of facilities and their proximity to neighborhoods. Proximity scores range from 0 to 10, with higher scores representing closer proximity. Information on active RMP facilities is pulled and updated for each new iteration of the EPA EJ Screen from the RMP National Program System. See more detailed information online.

SNAP Benefits 🔥💨 Social Determinants of Health Description: The percentage of individuals in a household receiving supplemental nutrition assistance program (SNAP) benefits. SNAP, also known as food stamps, provides nutritional benefits to supplement the food budget of families in need. To be eligible for SNAP, a household’s income and assets need to fall below a certain amount. See more detailed information about benefits and eligibility online. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Traffic Density 💨 Social Determinants of Health, Environmental Hazard Description: The average traffic density in vehicle kilometers per hour in 2017. Traffic density measures the concentration of vehicles on roads within an area. Source: CalEnviroScreen 4.0, 2021

Tree Canopy 🔥 Social Determinants of Health Description: The average percent of land covered by tree canopy in 2011. See more detailed information online. Source: California Healthy Places Index (HPI) 3.0, 2022

Uninsured Rate 🔥💨 Social Determinants of Health Description: The percentage of individuals who are not covered by any type of health insurance (i.e. public and private). Source: American Community Survey (ACS), 2018-2022 5-year Estimates

Workers in Heat-Exposed Occupations 🔥 Vulnerable Populations Description: The average percent of workers, aged 16 years and older, in occupations with a high exposure to extreme heat. According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, these include agriculture, construction, waste management, and warehousing. For more information on how we constructed this indicator, please refer to our technical report. Source: American Community Survey, 2018-2022 5-year Estimates
